# O Moinho — Site Briefing

## Business
O Moinho (The Windmill) is a family-owned Portuguese restaurant located in Ericeira, a charming coastal town known for its surfing culture and traditional fishing heritage. Established in 1987, the restaurant is housed in a beautifully restored 19th-century windmill overlooking the Atlantic Ocean.

The restaurant specializes in traditional Portuguese cuisine with a modern twist, focusing on fresh seafood sourced daily from local fishermen and seasonal ingredients from their own garden. Signature dishes include Cataplana de Marisco, Arroz de Lavagante, and a legendary Pastel de Nata made from a family recipe passed down three generations.

The target audience is a mix of Portuguese families celebrating special occasions, food-loving tourists exploring the Ericeira coastline, and local regulars who appreciate consistently excellent food in an unforgettable setting. The tone should be warm, inviting, and proud of tradition — but not stuffy. Think "your grandmother's cooking, elevated."

O Moinho has been featured in TimeOut Lisboa and Evasoes magazine, and holds a TripAdvisor Travellers' Choice award. They seat 60 inside (across two floors of the windmill) and 40 on the ocean-view terrace.

## Pages

A testimonial or press quote. Opening hours and a CTA to make a reservation. Maybe a small section about the ocean-view terrace.

- **Menu**: Full menu organized by categories: Starters (Entradas), Fish (Peixe), Meat (Carne), Desserts (Sobremesas), Drinks (Bebidas). Each item with name, short description, and price. Mention the daily fresh catch and seasonal specials. A note about dietary accommodations.

- **About**: The story of O Moinho — how the family acquired the windmill, the restoration process, three generations of recipes. Photos of the windmill then vs. now. Introduction to the chef (Maria Santos, 3rd generation). The restaurant's philosophy: local ingredients, traditional techniques, modern presentation. Awards and press mentions.

- **Gallery**: A photo gallery showcasing the restaurant interior (both floors), the terrace with ocean view, signature dishes, the kitchen, and the windmill exterior at different times of day. Use a lightbox layout.

- **Contact**: Reservation form (name, email, phone, date, time, party size, special requests). Map showing location. Opening hours (Tue-Sun 12:00-15:00, 19:00-22:30, closed Mondays). Directions from Lisbon (30 min from A8 motorway). Parking information.

Transparent header that becomes solid white on scroll. Logo on the left. Navigation links: Home, Menu, About, Gallery, Contact. A "Reserve" CTA button on the right (links to contact page). Language switcher (PT/EN) as a simple toggle. On mobile: hamburger menu with full-screen overlay.

## Footer
Three columns: (1) Logo + brief description + social media icons, (2) Quick links to all pages + "Reserve a Table" link, (3) Contact info + opening hours. Below: copyright line. Keep it elegant and minimal. Dark background with warm accent colors.

## Design Preferences
- Colors: Warm and earthy. Deep navy blue (#1B2A4A) as primary (represents the ocean), warm terracotta/burnt orange (#C4703F) as accent (represents the windmill's clay), cream/off-white (#FAF7F2) for backgrounds, dark charcoal (#2D2D2D) for text.
- Typography: Elegant serif for headings (Playfair Display), clean sans-serif for body text (Inter or Lato). The feel should be "refined but approachable."
- Mood: Mediterranean warmth meets Atlantic breeze. Elegant but not pretentious. The site should make you hungry and want to book a table immediately.
- Generous whitespace, subtle shadows, rounded corners. Photos should be large and atmospheric.

## Additional Notes
- The reservation form should be the main CTA throughout the site. Every page should have a path to making a reservation.

- The restaurant is wheelchair accessible on the ground floor and terrace — mention this somewhere.
- They offer a private dining room on the upper floor for events (up to 20 guests).
- During summer (June-September), they have live Fado music on Friday and Saturday evenings.
